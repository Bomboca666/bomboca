2010/2015

My journey started in 2010, when I was 19 years old. I didn't have any experience working, but started working in a factory, first as an assembler, but in a matter of months I started welding in the MIG/TIG process. Most of my time spent in that plant was welding catalysts on the night shift.

x

2015/2020

I've moved to the cold part of my plant and needed to adapt to a completely different environment. Instead of manual welding and robots, I was in a part of the plant where the benders came in, mostly Crippa. Huge machines that bend and cut pipe. I took my first steps with some G-code and started working as a team leader. It was a big change and some of the best years in that plant, and I worked four years on the day shift in this timeline.

x

2020/Present

Back to welding I am a team leader on one of the plant's oldest projects, the first project I worked on twelve years ago. I learned some days ago, by myself, how to correct trajectories in the welding robots by trial and error, mostly on Motoman and Fanuc. And four weeks ago (today, as I write, September six), I took my first steps into the technical world of the Internet, thanks to my sister-in-law. We spend every Saturday teaching me new and exciting things that I hope I will put to good use in the future. Besides everything, I'm a father, a companion, a worker, and a curious person who is eager to learn.
