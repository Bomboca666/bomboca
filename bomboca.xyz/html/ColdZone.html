Cold Zone

First Impressions

My first week was awful. There were a bunch of new mechanics and processes, and at first I didn't like the change, but I was transferred for health issues, so I needed to adapt to this new reality. At first, I worked on a bender and a forming (machine that gives shapes to tubes), and with time, I started to like the work, especially the benders. They have a lot more mechanics than a welding robot, and the programming was more accessible because the machine was unlocked, so everyone could change the programs of the designated references. I loved welding, but this was an entirely new universe, and I started to like it. The team leader that I had suffered a bike accident, and I became his substitute. When he recovered and returned, they kept me as a team leader in another shift, and finally I had more spare time to mess with the benders programming, mostly Crippa.

Best Years

Stress! The amount of stress was unbelievable. We produced parts for every corner of the plant, and shipped parts to other plants internationally. Running from one bender to the next was a typical day. We got along great with the supervisor during that shift. She was very methodical, which was great because it meant I could keep my team organized and make the parts that were needed for the week. But then, my manager asked me if I could form a team leader for the night shift, and I proposed that I move to the night shift and form a team leader during the day. He agreed. And with that, I parted ways with my regular team and returned to the night shift. The night shift is from midnight to 8 AM. I worked 12 hours a day for the next month to form a team leader in one shift and assist the another team leader in the other shift. It was intense and exhausting.
